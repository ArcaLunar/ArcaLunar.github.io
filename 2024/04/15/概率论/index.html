Remark 2.

The meaning of the C.I.: The C.I. obtained with confidence level β\betaβ has a probability of β\betaβ to cover the unknown mean μ\muμ. This is the range of values you expect your estimate to fall between if you redo your test, within a certain level of confidence.

Remark 3.

We should NOT say that μ\muμ has a probability of β\betaβ to be contained in the C.I. obtained: because μ\muμ is a fixed unknown constant and is NOT a random variable but the end points of the C.I. are random variables.

Remark 4.

Given the sample mean Xˉ\bar XXˉ, the population variance σ2\sigma^2σ2 and the confidence level β\betaβ, the larger the sample size nnn is, the shorter the C.I. will be and the length will tend to zero as nnn tends to infinity

Remark 5.

Given the sample mean, the population variance and the sample size, the larger the confidence level is, the longer the C.I. will be.
